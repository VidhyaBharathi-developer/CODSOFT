Tribute to Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru

"The architect of modern India"

Early Life & Background

Jawaharlal Nehru, India's first Prime Minister, was born on November 14, 1889, in Allahabad, India2. He came from a wealthy and politically influential family. His father, Motilal Nehru, was a prominent lawyer and nationalist leader, while his mother, Swarup Rani Nehru, played a significant role in his upbringing. Nehru received a Western-style education. He was homeschooled in India by English tutors before continuing his studies at Harrow School in London and Trinity College, Cambridge2. Later, he trained in law at the Inner Temple, becoming a barrister before returning to India. His exposure to British education and culture shaped his intellectual outlook, but he soon became deeply involved in India's struggle for independence. His early political career saw him joining the Indian National Congress, where he emerged as a progressive leader advocating for secularism, democracy, and modernization. His association with Mahatma Gandhi helped him rise within the party, eventually leading to his pivotal role in India's independence movement.

Major Achievements

  • India’s first Prime Minister (1947–1964)
  • Introduced Five-Year Plans for economic development
  • Founded prestigious institutions like AIIMS, IITs, and ISRO
  • Advocated for secularism, democracy, and scientific temperament
  • Introduced reforms granting Hindu women equal rights in inheritance and property
  • Positioned India as a neutral force during the Cold War
  • Established India's electoral system
  • Managed the rehabilitation of millions displaced by Partition
  • Signed a crucial water-sharing agreement with Pakistan

Legacy

  • Democracy & Secularism – Strengthened India's democratic and secular foundations.
  • Economic & Industrial Growth – Laid the groundwork for India's planned economy and scientific advancements.
  • Non-Alignment Policy – Kept India neutral during the Cold War.
  • Criticism & Controversy – Some argue his policies led to economic stagnation and strategic missteps.
  • Environmental Awareness – Encouraged conservation and sustainable development.

Important Dates

  • Birth Anniversary – November 14, 1889:
    Celebrated as a national remembrance of Nehru’s birth. It also marks the beginning of Children’s Day festivities across India.
  • Death Anniversary – May 27, 1964:
    A day of solemn tributes recognizing his contributions and leadership after India’s independence.
  • Children’s Day – November 14:
    Pandit Nehru’s love for children earned him the title “Chacha Nehru.” On this day, schools and communities organize fun events, cultural programs, and awards to honor children's creativity.
